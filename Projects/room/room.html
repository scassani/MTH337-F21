Is there room … ?¶

due: Friday, Oct. 1, 11:59 PM

Consider a circle \(\mathcal{C}\) with area \(\mathcal{A}\) and a collection of circles \(\{C_1,C_2,C_3,...\}\) with area

\[A_1>A_2>A_3>\ldots. \text{ and } \mathcal{A}>A_1\]

We would like to sequentially place each of this circles at random coordinates in such a way that

  • the circle is inside \(\mathcal{C}\)

  • the circle is disjoint from any of the other circles previously placed inside \(\mathcal{C}\)

The placement should (theoretically) be possible if \(A_1+A_2+A_3+\ldots\leq \mathcal{A}\).

For this project we will look at some specific formula for the relationshinp governing the area of the n-th circle. First we will choose a convergent series

\[\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\]

The area \(A_n\) will correspond to the n-th term of the series \(a_n\), and the area \(\mathcal{A}\) will be the sum of the series.

We will focus on convergent series that have an explicit formula for their sum, Geometric series and p-series.

  • Geometric series are convergent if \(p<1\) and their sum is equal to:

\[\sum_{n=1}^\infty p^n=\dfrac{p}{1-p}, \text{ for } p<1\]

For example, if \(p=\dfrac{1}{2}\), then \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^n=1\)

  • P-series are convergent if \(p>1\) and their sum is equal to the zeta function \(\zeta(p)\)

\[\sum_{n=1}^\infty \dfrac{1}{n^p}=\zeta(p), \text{ for } p>1\]

For example, if \(p=2\), then \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \dfrac{1}{n^2}=\zeta(2)\simeq 1.64493\)

Project¶

Explore how the value of p will affect the circle placement inside of \(\mathcal{C}\). Are there some values of the parameter for which the process seems to continue indefinetly? Analyze and give an explanation for the obtained results.
